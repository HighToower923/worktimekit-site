When to use this timesheet calculator

Use this page when you need to total a single shift and subtract a lunch break or other unpaid break minutes. It is a good fit for one day at a time, especially when you want both clock-style time and decimal hours for a timesheet or payroll check. If you need Monday through Sunday totals, the weekly timesheet calculator is the better choice because this page is not built for a full week of separate entries.

Example: A standard day shift from 9:00 AM to 5:30 PM with a 30-minute lunch gives 8h 00m of paid time. Example: An overnight shift from 10:00 PM to 6:30 AM with a 30-minute break gives 8h 00m of paid time and is handled automatically without manual next-day math.

How to calculate work hours using this timesheet calculator

  1. Enter your shift start time and end time.
  2. Add the total break minutes you do not want counted as paid work hours.
  3. Enter an hourly rate if you want a quick pay estimate.
  4. Click Calculate to total the timesheet.
  5. Review paid time, decimal hours, break deduction, and estimated pay.

Example of calculating work hours from a shift

How this timesheet hours calculation works

The calculator converts your start time and end time into minutes so the work hours are easy to compare. It subtracts break minutes from the total shift length and handles overnight timesheet entries when the end time falls after midnight. The remaining minutes are shown as hours and minutes, and also converted into decimal hours so you can calculate hours worked for payroll or reporting.

Common calculation scenarios

  • 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM shift with a 30-minute lunch break.
  • Late shift work hours that continue past midnight.
  • Timesheet hours with unpaid break deduction for payroll.
  • Quick shift hours calculation before submitting a daily timesheet.

Why accurate timesheet hours matter

Accurate hours worked help prevent payroll mistakes, underpaid breaks, and incorrect overtime totals. A clear employee timesheet also makes it easier to review total work hours before you submit a shift or compare payroll hours later.

FAQ

Questions about this timesheet calculator

These answers cover the most common issues users run into when they total shift hours and break deductions.

How does the break deduction work?

The calculator subtracts break minutes from the full shift so your work hours reflect paid time instead of total time on site. This makes the timesheet result easier to use for payroll checks, shift hours calculation, and daily hour tracking.

Can I use it for overnight shifts?

Yes. If your timesheet shift starts late in the day and ends after midnight, the calculator treats it as an overnight shift automatically. That helps you total work hours and calculate hours worked without adding the next day by hand.

Why show decimal hours too?

Many payroll systems and timesheet forms use decimal work hours such as 7.50 instead of 7h 30m. This calculator shows both formats so you can read the result quickly and still copy the number into another record when you calculate hours worked.

Where should I go for a whole week?

Use the weekly timesheet calculator when you need Monday through Sunday totals instead of a single shift. If your timesheet has several separate entries or dates, the timecard calculator may be even more useful for tracking work hours across multiple shifts.
