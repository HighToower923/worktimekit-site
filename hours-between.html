When to use this calculator

Use this page when you want a pure time difference between two points on the clock. It is useful for checking a daytime gap, an overnight gap, or the duration of an appointment, travel window, or simple shift span when you do not need break subtraction or payroll logic.

Example: 9:00 AM to 1:30 PM gives a daytime gap of 4h 30m. Example: 10:15 PM to 6:45 AM gives an overnight gap of 8h 30m. Example: 2:00 PM to 3:20 PM gives an appointment duration of 1h 20m.

How to calculate hours worked between two times

  1. Enter the start time.
  2. Enter the end time.
  3. Click Calculate difference to total the gap between the two times.
  4. Check the result in hours and minutes as well as decimal hours.
  5. Use Reset if you want to try another time range.

How this time difference calculation works

The calculator converts both times into minutes so the difference can be measured accurately. If the end time is earlier than the start time, it treats the result as an overnight calculation into the next day. This page does not apply break subtraction, and the final difference is converted into both hours and minutes and decimal hours so you can calculate hours worked more clearly.

Common calculation scenarios

  • 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM shift hours calculation.
  • Overnight work hours from late evening to early morning.
  • Simple timesheet hours without lunch break subtraction.
  • Appointment or travel time between two points in the day.

Why accurate time differences matter

When you calculate hours worked between two times, even a small mistake can change payroll hours, shift planning, or total work hours on an employee timesheet. A simple time difference calculator helps reduce manual counting errors, especially for overnight shifts.

FAQ

Questions about time differences

Many users search for an hours between times calculator to avoid manual counting. These answers explain what the tool does.

Does this subtract lunch or breaks?

No. This calculator only measures the raw time between the start and end points. If you want work hours with break subtraction, use the timesheet calculator instead for a fuller shift hours calculation.

Can it calculate overnight durations?

Yes. A range such as 11:30 PM to 4:15 AM is treated as crossing midnight, so the calculator counts the next day correctly. This is helpful for overnight work hours, travel blocks, late shifts, and other timesheet hours checks.

Why show decimal hours?

Decimal values are easier to transfer into a timesheet, payroll sheet, or work log, while the hours-and-minutes view is easier to read at a glance. This calculator gives both so you can use the format that fits your record when you calculate hours worked.

What tool should I use for a whole week?

Move to the weekly timesheet calculator when you need a Monday through Sunday total. If you want a more flexible calculator for several separate entries, the timecard calculator is also useful for timesheet hours across multiple shifts.
